Body Shaming and mental health

Body shaming is the action of expressing humiliation about another individual’s body shape or size which can result in severe emotional trauma, especially at a young age. Body shaming is serious and gives huge negative effects on the physical and mental health of a person.Being fat, being skinny, and having bodyweight that is not in harmony with their height are the common reasons why people body-shame others.This makes them feel uneasy or embarrassed in public situations where other people are concentrating on their appearance.

    Why do people body shame others?

    Generally, bodies come in various shapes and sizes. However, this doesn’t mean that people accidentally body shame others without realizing it.Furthermore, people who are overweight will be criticised no matter what clothes they wear or what actions they have done.Research shows that the use of Instagram is linked to a variety of body image concerns, including body dissatisfaction and self-objectification.Social media is easily accessible to all kinds of age and people, great emphasis on idealized body, skin, and type can be observed

    People today are blaming both men and women for their body types. Women are primarily chastised or mocked for their weight by being labeled "fat" or "skinny."Men's confidence is often associated with their physical characteristics such as their face, hair, and muscularity. Consequently, if men aren't muscular to even match the ideal version of men with "Perfect Bodies," they are subjected to body shaming.As the trends are thriving, only men with abs and built muscles are treated as handsome and fit. It has also been acclaimed that social media can play a part as a negative moderator between the muscular ideals that men have, which in turn leads to a negative image and evaluation of their own bodies in regard to societal comparisons.

    According to a reserch conducted by International Journal of Academic Research in Business and Social Sciences,being called fat, being skinny and having a body weight that is not in harmony with the height of their body are the statements that many of the respondents agreed with.This clearly shows the outer appearance of an individual is the major reason for people to body shame. Therefore, people who do not have an ideal body always feel insecure with themselves and have anxiety or depression as they continuously suffer from negative comments about their body

    Effects of body shaming

    Being commented negatively on your appearance will impact negatively to both mental and physical aspects of one's life.Some of the issues that may develop due to body shaming include:

  1. Social anxiety When one is publicly bullied, the natural response would be to avoid putting themselves in those situations This can culminate into isolating oneself and avoiding social interaction
  2. Depression:Increase in feelings of low self-image,self-worth and self-esteem often can result into a state of depression
  3. Eating disorders: People suffering from an eating disorder may believe that controlling their food intake will change their appearance and make the body shaming stop.The eating disoders include anorexia and bulimia.
Remember, suicide prevention is everyone's responsibility. If you or someone you know is in crisis, contact a suicide prevention hotline or seek immediate medical attention.

    IF YOU HAVE BEEN BODY SHAMED...

    If you have experienced body shaming first hand, remember that you are not alone and that you are in control of how you respond. Try taking the higher road by implementing some of these ideas:

  1. Do not change your appearance because of what someone else said or thinks
  2. Talk to those you are close with for emotional support
  3. Reach out to a therapist to talk about your emotions and seek guidance
  4. Practice self-compassion
  5. Practice forgiveness
